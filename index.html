Police overtime is low this year. But it’s still way over budget.

The Independent Budget Office has released a report detailing the New York Police Department’s overtime spending, and the department has a long way to go to fiscal responsibility.

By Molly Boigon

October 7, 2021

A New York Police Department patrol vehicle. Credit: André Gustavo Stumpf/Wikimedia Commons

More overtime

The New York Police Department is slated to spend about $430 on overtime this year, about half of last year’s $848 million.

Fewer events

Fewer parades and other events, less traffic and fewer after-school programs because of the pandemic have contributed to the decline in overtime spending.

Fewer officers

The size of the force is also declining. There were 5% fewer uniformed officers in January 2021 than there were the previous year.

Over budget

The city’s financial plan is providing for even fewer officers over the next five years. Despite these factors, the police department is still over budget on overtime spending. Only $268 million was allocated for this year’s overtime budget.
